First, let us take a look to the math needed in this subject.
The actual math consists mainly of basic linear algebra.
A general knowledge of quantum phisics is a great advantage too.

In quantum computation we use, instead of the regular bits wich have either the state 1 or 0, so called "qubits", wich are the superposition of the sate 1 and 0. A quantum state $$ \ket{\phi} $$ is described as 
    $$ \ket{\phi} $$ = $$ \alpha \ket{0} + \beta  \ket{1} $$

where $$ \ket{0} && and $$ \ket{1} $$ are just two dimensional base vectors with
$$ \ket{0} = \mqty{1 \\ 0} $$
