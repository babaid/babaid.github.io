Definition: Let $$ \\mathrm{V}$$ be a $$ \\mathbb{K}  $$ Vectorspace. A mapping $$ \\mathscr{F}$$ : $$ \\mathrm{V} \\rightarrow \\mathbb{K} $$ is called a "Functional".  
In the following let $$ \\mathbb{K}=\\mathbb{R} $$.

Let's see an example:  
$$ \\mathrm{V} = $$ { x $$ \\epsilon $$ $$ \\mathscr{C}^1(\[ 0, 1\])|x(0)= x(1) = 0} $$  
$$ \\mathscr{F} (x) = \\int\\limits_0^1 \\sqrt{1+\\dot{x}^2}: \\mathrm{d}x $$

As you can see $$ \\mathscr{F} $$ is a function of a function. You will see later on some more physical examples.
