Hello and Welcome to another article!

First, let us take a look to the math needed in this subject.
The actual math consists mainly of basic linear algebra.
A general knowledge of quantum phisics is a great advantage too.

In quantum computation we use, instead of the regular bits wich have either the state 1 or 0, so called "qubits", wich are the superposition of the sate 1 and 0. A quantum state $$ \| \phi \rangle $$ is described as 
$$ \| \phi \rangle  =  \alpha \|0 \rangle + \beta  \|1 \rangle $$
where $$ \|0 \rangle $$  and $$ \|1 \rangle $$ are just two dimensional base vectors with
$$ \|0 \rangle = \maqty{1 \\ 0} $$ and $$ \|1 \rangle = \mqty{0 \\ 1} $$ , $$\|0 \rangle, \|1 \rangle \in \mathbb{C}^2 $$
